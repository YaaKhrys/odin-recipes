Jollof

This delicious looking jollof goes with nicely fried chicken with pepper sauce and fried plantain. Want to know how this magic was made? See for yourself!

Ingredient List

The Main Dish - Jollof Rice

  • 2 cups of long-grain rice
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil or palm oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers or habanero peppers, finely chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 can (400 grams) of tomato paste or 4 large tomatoes, blended into a puree
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder, dried thyme, paprika, ground ginger, ground nutmeg, dried bay leaves, ground allspice, dried rosemary, and ground cloves
  • 2 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 pound (450 grams) of chicken, beef, or shrimp for added protein
  • Optional: Mixed vegetables such as carrots, peas, and bell peppers

The Fried Chicken

  • 2-3 pounds (about 1-1.5 kg) of chicken pieces (such as drumsticks, thighs, or chicken breasts)
  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of dried herbs (such as thyme, oregano, or basil)
  • 1 tablespoon of salt (adjust to taste)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

The Fried Plantain

  • Ripe plantains (choose ones that are yellow with some black spots for best flavor)
  • Vegetable oil for frying (enough to cover the bottom of the pan)
  • Optional: Salt
  • Optional: Ground cinnamon or nutmeg
  • Optional: Cayenne pepper or chili powder (for a spicy kick)

The Steps To Prepare The Dish

The Main Dish - Jollof Rice

  1. Heat vegetable oil or palm oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions and minced garlic. Saut√© until onions become translucent.
  2. Add tomato paste or blended tomatoes to the pot. Stir well and cook for a few minutes until the raw flavor of the tomatoes dissipates.
  3. Add spices such as curry powder, thyme, paprika, ginger, nutmeg, bay leaves, allspice, rosemary, and cloves. Mix well to combine the flavors.
  4. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water, then add it to the pot. Stir until the rice is evenly coated with the tomato mixture.
  5. Pour in chicken or vegetable broth, and add coconut milk to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir gently to combine all the ingredients.
  6. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and simmer over low heat for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and tender, and the liquid is absorbed. Avoid stirring the rice too often to prevent it from becoming sticky.

The Fried Chicken

  1. If marinating the chicken, mix hot sauce, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or skillet over medium heat.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and dried herbs together.
  4. Take each marinated chicken piece and coat it in the seasoned flour mixture, ensuring it is fully coated.
  5. Carefully place the coated chicken pieces into the hot oil and fry them until golden brown and crispy on both sides. This usually takes about 6-8 minutes per side, depending on the size of the chicken pieces. Cook in batches if needed.
  6. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

The Fried Plantain

  1. Peel the ripe plantains and cut them into diagonal slices, about 1/2-inch thick.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Carefully add the plantain slices to the hot oil and fry them until golden brown and caramelized on both sides. This usually takes about 3-4 minutes per side.

Arrange the jollof rice, fried chicken, and fried plantains on a serving platter or individual plates. Serve them together as a delicious and complete meal.

Enjoy your homemade jollof rice with fried chicken and plantain!
